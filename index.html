REVOLUTIONARY IDEAS START HERE

Penn’s inspired liberal arts curriculum, sweeping the humanities, natural and social sciences, and quantitative study, develops your analytical and writing skills, and encourages you to reimagine the possibilities for positive change in local and global contexts.

YOUR NETWORK: LOCAL TO GLOBAL

A unified campus means you can easily study across Penn’s four undergraduate schools and select graduate programs while accessing research centers, cultural hubs, and teaching hospitals. Start on campus to connect to the world: the Penn community offers brilliant internship and academic programs abroad.

AT HOME IN PHILADELPHIA

Penn’s campus is tucked into a thriving world-class city with a quirky vibe. From a vibrant arts and culture scene to myriad internships and volunteer opportunities across the arts, social sciences, and medicine, this is a space that will inspire you to create.

THE COLLEGE OF ARTS AND SCIENCES

YOUR ACADEMIC HOME

The College is the cornerstone of a Penn education: the academic home for the entire University. With over 55 majors and 2,000 courses, it’s up to you to decide how you want to use this intellectual array to pursue your interests. Your journey will be flexible yet structured, covering wide-ranging topics and encouraging in-depth exploration and research.

CONNECT KNOWLEDGE AND IMPACT

Let your curiosity guide your research, leadership, and activism on campus. Translate knowledge into change while working with campus hubs including the Center for Research and Fellowships (CURF), Perry World House, Kelly Writers House, the Civic House, and more. Your academic advisors and on-campus mentors are here to help you along.

YOUR FUTURE, REDEFINED

A College education is powerful: not only will you master critical thinking, analysis, and writing, you will be ready to share your perspective and implement change. This flexible and rigorous education prepares you for excellence in a constantly changing world that demands empathetic leadership.

Learning At Penn

Start at:

THE SCHOOL OF NURSING

STUDY NURSING AT THE HIGHEST LEVEL

Transform patient care, try out healthcare management, and study global health initiatives. You can implement change in varied healthcare settings through Penn Nursing’s rigorous and dynamic education that emphasizes mentorship, hands-on learning, and study across the liberal arts.

PROVIDE CARE AS AN UNDERGRADUATE

Deliver high-quality care in top-ranked teaching facilities during your studies at Penn Nursing. Students practice and refine their skills during their three years (or 896 hours) of clinical experience in Philadelphia with additional opportunities to pursue study and clinical rotations abroad.

TREMENDOUS OPPORTUNITY POST-GRADUATION

As healthcare expands and intersects with other industries, Nursing graduates find limitless opportunities in clinical, policy, and nonprofit settings. Penn Nursing cultivates strong mentorship relationships to help students decide where to lead next. The majority of nurses go deeper into their education: 80% of Penn Nursing graduates enroll in one of 15 master’s degree programs at Penn.

Learning At Penn

THE WHARTON SCHOOL OF BUSINESS

EXPERIENCE THE WORLD'S FIRST BUSINESS SCHOOL

Personalize your learning to pursue your unique interests in over 20 concentrations led by industry-changing professors. Wharton blends business with the liberal arts to create a powerful education that responds to your passions and goals.

PRACTICE TEAM-BUILDING AND LEADERSHIP

Experience meaningful team-building opportunities outside the classroom. Wharton students practice self-development through leadership-focused programs and lectures, as well as participation in Wharton clubs and activities. Discover how you will lead.

THE WORLD IS WAITING

Go anywhere and do everything. Wharton students pursue jobs at top global companies, become leaders in nonprofit and the arts, or continue entrepreneurial ventures. An influential network of Wharton alums support graduates as they advance their careers across industries with an inclination towards economic and social well-being.

THE SCHOOL OF ENGINEERING

JOIN A PIONEERING PROGRAM

Study and create innovations that define modernity. Featuring a flexible and multidisciplinary curriculum, Penn Engineering encourages students to develop expertise in a breadth of technology-based fields from electrical engineering and computer science to networked and social systems engineering.

PURSUE SCIENTIFIC DISCOVERY

Engineering at Penn is naturally hands-on: students conduct research through the Rachleff Scholars Program, Clark Scholars Program, and the many centers and institutes on campus, including the General Robotics, Automation, Sensing, and Perception (GRASP) Lab and the Singh Center for Nanotechnology. Extracurricular experiences, from building electric racecars to developing software for nonprofits, reinforce Penngineers’ innovative approach to challenges.

ENDLESS CAREER OPTIONS

Create a network. Pursue a PhD in artificial intelligence. Contribute to drug development and pharmaceutical research. Become a digital storyteller. Penn engineers are brilliant thinkers and ready doers. In fact, 96% of Penn Engineering students are meaningfully employed or continuing their studies after graduation (2014 Career Plans Survey).

COSTS & FINANCIAL AID

University of Pennsylvania is committed to making its practical, powerful, and flexible Ivy League education available to the most talented and hardworking students. If admitted, Penn will meet your family’s full demonstrated need. Our financial aid policy means that you will live and learn with a truly diverse body of fellow students and friends from all 50 states and more than 100 countries—each one bringing a distinctive voice to Penn’s campus.
